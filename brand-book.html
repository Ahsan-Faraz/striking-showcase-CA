01 — Identity

Logo

The Striking Showcase logo is the cornerstone of the brand. It must always be reproduced clearly and consistently.

Logo Versions

White — Dark Backgrounds
Maroon — Light Backgrounds

Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the star mark in the logo. No other graphic elements, text, or edge boundaries should intrude into this space.

X X X X

Minimum Size

To ensure legibility, never reproduce the logo smaller than these minimum dimensions.

Print: 1.5 in / 38mm

Digital: 120px wide

Favicon: 32px (mark only)

06 — Language

Brand Voice

Striking Showcase speaks with authority and warmth. We know the sport, we respect the grind, and we cut straight to the point.

Voice Principles

Confident

We know our value. Bold statements, never hedging or apologizing.

Direct

No fluff, no jargon walls. Say it clearly, say it once.

Authoritative

Sports authority meets tech precision. Data-driven, credible.

Peer-Level

We speak to college coaches as equals, not up to them or down.

Do vs. Don't

✓ Do

"Verified stats. Highlight reels. Direct messaging. Everything you need to find your next recruit."

✗ Don't

"We humbly hope to perhaps provide some assistance in your recruiting journey, if that's okay with you."

✓ Do

"2,400+ athletes. 215 average. Zero cost for coaches."

✗ Don't

"We have a considerable number of talented young athletes who might be of interest to your program's needs."

Iconography

Usage Guidelines

  • ★ Use the star as section dividers between content areas — flanked by gradient lines
  • ★ Use the star inside verified badges and pricing card headers
  • ★ Use ghost (low opacity) stars as hero backdrop decoration
  • ★ Bowling pin silhouettes appear at low opacity in hero sections and stat bands
  • ★ Lane arrows can be used as directional elements, progress indicators, or subtle background texture — center arrow filled (maroon), flanking arrows outlined with fading opacity
  • ★ Pair lane arrows with range finder dots below for the full lane reference
  • ★ Never use icons as primary content — they are always supporting decoration
08 — Imagery

Photography

Photography should feel real, warm, and confident. Every image reinforces the brand's authenticity.

Photo Style Guide

  • Warm tones that complement maroon and gold accents
  • Bowling alley settings — lanes, pins, scoring areas
  • Confident, natural poses — never stiff or overly posed
  • Authentic moments: mid-throw, celebrating, strategizing
  • Good natural or ambient lighting — avoid harsh flash
  • Never use generic stock photography
  • Athletes should look focused, determined, and proud
  • Background depth of field keeps focus on the athlete
Color Treatment

Photos on dark backgrounds may receive a subtle maroon overlay (rgba(102,0,51,0.1)) to unify warm tones with the brand palette. Never desaturate or apply cold filters.
